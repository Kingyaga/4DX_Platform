# 4DX Platform Guide

## Overview

4DX Platform is a web application for running the 4 Disciplines of Execution inside an organization. It helps teams define Wildly Important Goals, track lead measures, review progress on a scoreboard, log activity, approve results, and keep a weekly cadence of accountability.

The platform is organized around three user groups:

- **Org Admins** manage the organization, teams, users, invites, and execution health.
- **Team Leads** create WIGs, define lead measures, approve activity, review reports, and run the weekly execution rhythm.
- **Team Members** view the scoreboard, log activity against lead measures, and complete weekly commitments.

## Core Concepts

### Wildly Important Goal (WIG)

A WIG is the most important outcome a team is trying to move. It has a clear starting point, target, deadline, and status. WIGs can be drafted, activated, and later closed as achieved, missed, or abandoned.

### Lag Measure

The lag measure is the result the team wants to improve. It answers the question: "What final outcome are we trying to move?"

Examples:

- Increase revenue from 500,000 to 750,000 by September 30.
- Reduce customer response time from 24 hours to 6 hours by quarter end.
- Complete a major operational milestone by a fixed deadline.

### Lead Measure

Lead measures are the behaviors or activities that influence the WIG. They should be measurable, repeatable, and owned by specific people.

Examples:

- Make 40 qualified sales calls per week.
- Complete 15 customer follow-ups per day.
- Publish weekly implementation status updates.

### Scoreboard

The scoreboard shows whether the team is winning. It displays WIG progress, lead measure progress, days remaining, and owner contributions based on approved activity.

### Weekly Session

The weekly session keeps the team accountable. Members review commitments, blockers, progress, and next actions.

### Activity Log

The activity log is where members submit work against lead measures. Submitted activity may require team lead approval before it affects the scoreboard.

## How To Use The Platform

## Signing In

1. Open the application and sign in with your email and password.
2. If your account was created with a temporary password, set a new password before continuing.
3. The app redirects you to the right dashboard based on your role.

## First-Time Tutorial

When a user opens the dashboard for the first time, the platform shows a guided tour. The tour is role-aware, so admins, team leads, and members see the workflow that matches their permissions.

All tutorials introduce:

- The 4DX workspace purpose.
- The notification center.
- The sidebar navigation.
- The main work area.

Member tutorials focus on:

- Reading the scoreboard.
- Logging lead measure activity.
- Submitting work for approval.
- Tracking pending and recent activity logs.
- Using weekly sessions.
- Reviewing team members and account settings.

Team lead tutorials focus on:

- Reviewing the team lead dashboard.
- Creating WIGs.
- Understanding current and closed WIGs.
- Adding and managing lead measures.
- Logging activity where assigned.
- Approving or declining activity requests.
- Running weekly sessions.
- Managing members and reports.

Admin tutorials focus on:

- Reviewing organization execution health.
- Creating and managing teams.
- Creating users.
- Generating and tracking invites.
- Reviewing organization activity.

Users can skip the tutorial at any time. Skipping marks that role's tutorial as complete for the current user. Users can replay the correct tutorial for their current role from the **Tutorial** button in the dashboard sidebar.

## Admin Workflow

Admins use the platform to prepare the operating environment.

1. Open the Admin Dashboard to review organization execution health.
2. Create and manage teams from the Teams page.
3. Add users to the organization and assign them to teams.
4. Generate invites when new users need access.
5. Review organization activity for audit and visibility.
6. Monitor teams that are at risk, behind, or missing execution cadence.

## Team Lead Workflow

Team leads turn strategy into execution.

1. Select the correct active team from the sidebar.
2. Open WIGs and create a new WIG in draft status.
3. Add the lag measure details, target, unit, and deadline.
4. Add lead measures and assign owners.
5. Activate the WIG when it is ready for the team.
6. Review submitted activity from the Requests page.
7. Approve valid activity so it updates the scoreboard.
8. Use Team Reports to inspect execution trends.
9. Close the WIG when the goal is complete or the deadline has passed.

## Team Member Workflow

Team members use the platform to stay focused and accountable.

1. Open the Scoreboard to see the current WIG and team progress.
2. Open Activity Log to submit activity against assigned lead measures.
3. Track whether submitted activity is pending, approved, or declined.
4. Join the Weekly Session to review commitments and blockers.
5. Check notifications for approvals, reminders, ownership changes, and WIG alerts.

## Notifications

The notification center shows important updates such as:

- Weekly sessions that are ready or overdue.
- Activity approvals and declines.
- WIGs that are at risk.
- WIG deadlines that have passed.
- Lead measure ownership changes.
- New or closed WIGs.

Unread notifications appear on the bell icon in the sidebar. Users can mark one notification as read or mark all notifications as read.

## Dashboard Navigation

Available pages depend on the user's role.

### Shared Team Pages

- **Scoreboard:** View active WIG progress and lead measure performance.
- **WIGs:** Create, configure, activate, and close WIGs where permitted.
- **Activity Log:** Submit and review activity logs.
- **Weekly Session:** Complete cadence-of-accountability sessions.
- **Members:** View and manage team membership where permitted.
- **Settings:** Manage user profile and password.

### Team Lead Pages

- **Team Lead Dashboard:** Review team execution status.
- **Requests:** Approve or decline pending activity.
- **Team Reports:** Inspect WIG and activity performance.

### Admin Pages

- **Dashboard:** Review organization-level execution health.
- **Teams:** Create teams and manage team members.
- **Users:** Manage organization users.
- **Invites:** Create and manage invite access.
- **Org Activity:** Review audit and activity history.

## Best Practices

- Keep WIGs few and focused. A team should know exactly what matters most.
- Use lead measures that people can influence every week.
- Keep the scoreboard visible and easy to understand.
- Log activity consistently, preferably close to when the work happens.
- Approve or decline activity promptly so the scoreboard stays trusted.
- Use weekly sessions to remove blockers and renew commitments.
- Treat notifications as execution signals, not general messages.

## Common Questions

### Why is my scoreboard not changing?

The scoreboard reflects approved activity. If activity is still pending, it appears in the activity log but does not change WIG progress yet.

### Why can I not see a team dashboard?

Your account may not have been assigned to a team. An admin or team lead needs to add you to the correct team.

### Why can I not create a WIG?

Only users with the right team role can create or activate WIGs. If you need access, ask an admin or team lead to update your role.

### What should I do when a WIG is at risk?

Review the scoreboard, inspect lead measure performance, identify the gap, and use the next weekly session to create specific recovery commitments.

## Support Notes For Operators

- Ensure users are assigned to the correct organization and team.
- Confirm team role when a user cannot access team lead features.
- Keep invite links controlled and revoke unused access when needed.
- Review activity and audit logs when execution data looks unexpected.
- Encourage teams to define lead measures before activating WIGs.
