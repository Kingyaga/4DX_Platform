1. Overview
1.1 Problem Statement
Teams within the organization set ambitious goals each quarter and year, but most goals quietly fade by week three. The "whirlwind" — the urgent operational demands of daily work — consumes the bandwidth needed for strategic execution. Existing tools (spreadsheets, OKR templates, project trackers) capture goals but do not enforce the discipline of executing them. Managers run inconsistent check-ins. Lead indicators are confused with lag indicators. Scoreboards become stale. Accountability becomes performative.

The result: strategy gets written, not executed.

1.2 Vision
An internal web platform that operationalizes the 4 Disciplines of Execution methodology as software-enforced behavior. The platform is not a tracker — it is a discipline engine. It makes it structurally difficult to drift, structurally easy to stay accountable, and structurally visible whether the team is winning or losing.

1.3 Goals
•	Force strategic focus by capping goals (max 2 WIGs per team)
•	Enforce the distinction between lead and lag measures
•	Make the scoreboard the default surface, not a buried report
•	Make the weekly accountability cadence unavoidable
•	Provide leadership with cross-team visibility into execution health

1.4 Non-Goals (v1)
•	Public/external sale of the platform
•	Mobile native apps (responsive web is sufficient)
•	AI-driven coaching or insight generation
•	Third-party integrations (Microsoft Teams, Jira, etc.)
•	Personal/individual (non-team) goal tracking
•	Executive portfolio analytics beyond a basic cross-team view

1.5 Success Metrics

Metric	Target (3 months post-launch)
Weekly session completion rate	≥ 80% per active team
Active WIGs per team	1–2 (no team exceeds the cap)
Lead measure activity logging cadence	≥ 70% of expected entries logged on time
Teams onboarded	3+ teams running active WIGs
WIG achievement rate	≥ 50% of WIGs reach their target by deadline

2. Users & Roles
2.1 Personas
Org Admin
Sets up the organization, manages teams, oversees execution health across teams. Likely a founder, COO, or operations lead.

Team Lead
Defines team WIGs and lead measures, runs the weekly cadence, holds members accountable. Owns the team's execution discipline.

Team Member
Executes against assigned lead measures, logs activity, completes weekly sessions, makes commitments.

3. Core Concepts (Domain Model)
3.1 Organization
The top-level container. One per deployment in v1, but architected for multi-org from day one.

3.2 Team
A group of people with a Team Lead and Members. Owns its own WIGs.

3.3 WIG (Wildly Important Goal)
A goal expressed as "Move [metric] from [X] to [Y] by [date]." Belongs to one Team. Maximum 2 active WIGs per team enforced by the system.

States:  Draft → Active → Achieved | Missed | Abandoned

3.4 Lead Measure
A predictive, influenceable behavior or output that drives the WIG. Each WIG has 2–3 lead measures. Each lead measure has a defined cadence (weekly target), unit, and one or more owners.

3.5 Activity Log
A record of lead measure activity by a team member. Includes value, date, and optional note.

3.6 Weekly Session
A structured weekly accountability ritual per WIG, per team member. Composed of three gated steps:

1.	Account — Status of last week's commitments
2.	Review — Scoreboard snapshot acknowledgment
3.	Commit — 1–3 specific commitments for the coming week

3.7 Commitment
A specific, schedulable action a team member commits to in a Weekly Session. Marked complete/incomplete the following week.

4. Functional Requirements
4.1 Authentication & Onboarding
•	FR-1.1  Users sign up via email + password (and/or SSO in a later release)
•	FR-1.2  First user to sign up becomes the Org Admin and creates the organization
•	FR-1.3  Org Admin invites users via email link; invitee sets password on first login
•	FR-1.4  Users can belong to one or more teams
•	FR-1.5  Users have a default landing team if they belong to multiple

4.2 Team Management
•	FR-2.1  Org Admin can create teams with name, description, Team Lead assignment
•	FR-2.2  Team Lead can add/remove members from their team
•	FR-2.3  Each team has a unique URL slug
•	FR-2.4  Teams cannot be deleted while they have active WIGs (must archive first)

4.3 WIG Creation & Management
•	FR-3.1  WIG creation uses a guided wizard with required fields: Title, Starting value (X), Target value (Y), Unit, Deadline, and optional Description
•	FR-3.2  System enforces a hard cap of 2 active WIGs per team. The "Create WIG" button is disabled with a tooltip when the cap is reached
•	FR-3.3  WIGs cannot be created without all required fields. The phrase "From X to Y by When" is shown live as the user fills the form
•	FR-3.4  WIGs can be edited only by the WIG owner (Team Lead) or Org Admin
•	FR-3.5  WIGs have a status field with controlled transitions: Draft → Active → (Achieved | Missed | Abandoned). Once closed, read-only

4.4 Lead Measure Management
•	FR-4.1  Each WIG must have at least 1 and at most 3 lead measures defined before activation
•	FR-4.2  Each lead measure has: Name, Description, Cadence (weekly | bi-weekly), Target value per cadence, Unit, Owner(s)
•	FR-4.3  System shows a coaching tooltip distinguishing leads (predictive, controllable behaviors) from lags (outcome metrics) — non-blocking
•	FR-4.4  Lead measures can be edited by the WIG owner; historical activity logs remain attached

4.5 Activity Logging
•	FR-5.1  Members can log activity against any lead measure they own
•	FR-5.2  Activity log entry: value (numeric), date (defaults to today), optional note
•	FR-5.3  Activity logs are visible to all team members for transparency
•	FR-5.4  Log entries can be edited within 24 hours; after that, immutable (corrections require an offsetting entry)

4.6 Scoreboard
•	FR-6.1  The team scoreboard is the default landing page after login
•	FR-6.2  Scoreboard displays per active WIG: title, current vs target progress bar, days remaining, and trend indicator (on track / at risk / off track)
•	FR-6.3  Per lead measure: weekly target vs actual, per-owner contribution, 4-week trend sparkline, and status color (green ≥100%, amber 75–99%, red <75%)
•	FR-6.4  Scoreboard auto-refreshes every 60 seconds while the tab is open
•	FR-6.5  Scoreboard has a "Display Mode" — full-screen view optimized for a wall display

4.7 Weekly Session (Discipline 4 — Critical Path)
•	FR-7.1  A Weekly Session is generated automatically every Monday at 00:00 (org-configurable timezone) for each active WIG, per team member
•	FR-7.2  Members are notified via in-app banner that their session is due
The session is a 3-step gated flow:

Step 1 — Account
•	System displays last week's commitments
•	User marks each: Done | Partially Done | Not Done
•	If not done, user must select a reason (Whirlwind / Misjudged effort / Blocked / Other) and add a brief reflection

Step 2 — Review
•	System shows the team's scoreboard snapshot for the week
•	User must acknowledge before proceeding

Step 3 — Commit
•	User enters 1–3 commitments for the coming week
•	Each commitment is free text, with an optional linked lead measure
•	Commitments must be specific (system warns on vague entries < 5 words)

•	FR-7.4  Session is only marked complete when all 3 steps are submitted
•	FR-7.5  Until the session is complete, the user sees a persistent banner on every page
•	FR-7.6  Sessions have a deadline (default: end of Monday). Sessions completed late are flagged
•	FR-7.7  Team Lead sees a session completion roster — who has completed, who hasn't

4.8 Cross-Team View (Org Admin only)
•	FR-8.1  Org Admin sees a portfolio dashboard with active WIG count, WIG health summary, session completion rate, and lead measure hit rate per team
•	FR-8.2  Org Admin can drill into any team's scoreboard
•	FR-8.3  Org Admin cannot edit team-level data — view-only

4.9 Notifications
•	FR-9.1  Email notifications: invite to join, weekly session due (Monday morning), overdue (Tuesday morning if incomplete), WIG achieved/closed
•	FR-9.2  In-app notifications mirror the above
•	FR-9.3  Users can configure notification preferences in their profile

5. Non-Functional Requirements

Category	Requirement

Availability	99.5% uptime target
Security	All data encrypted at rest and in transit; passwords hashed (bcrypt/argon2)
Browser support	Latest 2 versions of Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox

8. Technical Architecture

Layer	Recommendation	Rationale
Frontend	Next.js 14+ (App Router) + TypeScript	SSR, file-based routing, full-stack in one repo
Styling	Tailwind CSS + shadcn/ui	Fast, professional, accessible primitives
Backend	Next.js API routes + tRPC	Type-safe API, single repo
Database	PostgreSQL (via Supabase or self-hosted)	Relational fit, mature, free tier available
ORM	Prisma or Drizzle	Type-safe DB access
Auth	NextAuth.js or Supabase Auth	Email/password, OAuth-ready
Email	Resend or Postmark	Transactional email for invites + reminders
Background jobs	Vercel Cron or node-cron	Weekly session generation, overdue notifications
Hosting	Vercel (frontend) + Supabase (DB)	Zero-config, generous free tier
Monitoring	Sentry	Error tracking

This stack lets a solo developer ship the platform in 6–10 weeks of focused work and supports future Microsoft Teams integration without a rewrite.

10. Risks & Mitigations

Risk	Likelihood	Impact	Mitigation
Teams won't run the weekly session	High	Critical	Make session a hard gate; surface team lead non-completion to Org Admin
Lead measures are actually lag measures	High	High	In-app coaching, examples by team type, future AI review
Scoreboard goes stale from non-logging	Medium	High	Daily logging reminders; team lead visibility into who logs
Whirlwind absorbs the platform itself	Medium	Critical	Keep activity logging under 30 seconds; aggressive UX simplicity
Adoption stalls after first team	Medium	Medium	Each team's success becomes the proof case for the next
Manager doesn't enforce discipline	High	Critical	Out of platform's control — document as known dependency
